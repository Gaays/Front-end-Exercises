Claude Shannon

the father of information theory
克劳德·艾尔伍德·香农

Here's a time line of Claude Shannon's life:

  • 1916-Born in Petoskey,Michigan
  • 1932-Graduating from Gaylord High School
  • 1936-Graduated in 1936 with two bachelor's degrees: one in electrical engineering and the other in mathematics.Then work on Vannevar Bush's differential analyzer, an early analog computer.
  • 1938-Writes his master's degree thesis, A Symbolic Analysis of Relay and Switching Circuits.[10] A paper from this thesis was published in 1938.
  • 1940-Received PhD from MIT
  • 1949-Proves that the cryptographic one-time pad is unbreakable.He also proved that any unbreakable system must have essentially the same characteristics as the one-time pad: the key must be truly random, as large as the plaintext, never reused in whole or part, and be kept secret.
  • 1956-Joins the MIT faculty to work in the Research Laboratory of Electronics (RLE).
  • 2001-Dies at the age of 84, on February 24, 2001
